My approach to building teams in new regions

1. Talent Strategy
2. Process & Collaboration
3. Market Understanding
4. Employer Brand
1

Talent Strategy and Org Design

- Founding team DNA: Autonomous builders comfortable with ambiguity and able to influence culture.
- Clarify org design and which leadership roles are needed early on.
- Identify which roles to unlock first (typically a top down approach).
- If leadership sit in the US our first hire will be a key hire for the team - they'll ideally be a senior hire and have a player coach mentality.
- Build a repeatable sourcing engine (outbound, referrals, warm intro's, inbound).
2

Process and Collaboration Across Timezones

- Build processes optimised for a-sync + timezone differences. (Kick-off documents, interview plans, rubrics, clear SLAs).
- Keep velocity high despite time-zone gaps: planned interview loops, predictable scheduling.
- Ensure consistent processes where possible across US + UK.
- Know when to escalate immediately
- Schedule weekly meetings / standing syncs
- Weekly reporting for hiring managers + execs.
3

Market Understanding

- Understand compensation norms across UK/EU.
- Factor in employment law differences: notice periods, holidays, parental leave etc
- Know how talent pools vary across EMEA and the implications for sourcing (consider language requirements).
- Market competitiveness and speed (some markets move slower than others)
- Candidate behaviour and cultural nuances: communication style, interview style, typical motivators)
- Clear understanding of competitors in the region and market trends.



4

Employer Brand in a New Region

- Craft a compelling narrative: "Founding team for EMEA HQ"
- Tell the story
- Run recruiting events or just meet people in person!
- Attend / sponsor conferences
- Localise messaging
- Build relationships (social groups, events)
- Capture and share wins
